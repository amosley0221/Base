A short history
01 — Ochre

The red of the cave.

Ground from iron-rich earth, red ochre stained the walls of Lascaux some 40,000 years before paint had a name.

02 — Vermilion

Worth more than gold.

Mercury and sulfur fused into a red so brilliant that emperors hoarded it and forgers were put to death for faking it.

03 — Carmine

An empire built on beetles.

Crushed cochineal from the New World dyed the robes of cardinals and the coats of armies — and quietly reshaped trade.
